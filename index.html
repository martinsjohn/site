Academic Background

I have a background in Electrical & Computer Engineering, with a B.S. and M.S. from Carnegie Mellon University. My past work involves using machine learning and computer vision sensing systems to understand human-machine interactions in advanced manufacturing environment.

As I look towards my future research career, I draw inspiration from living & infrastructure systems, traditional ecological knowledge, and modern computational advancements. I often wonder "How can we draw upon wisdom from living systems and established practices to build a more sustainable world?" and believe firmly that many solutions and keys to advancement are hidden within the very natural world that surrounds us.

Notable Past Projects

Human-Machine Interaction Dataset for Advanced Manufacturing

As part of the Sustainable And Livable Urban Systems (SALUS) Lab at Carnegie Mellon after my masters, I collected a large dataset of human-machine interactions in advanced manufacturing scenarios for training machine learning models and characterizing machinery use.

Smart Thermostat

For the final project for 12-770 Autonomous Buildings course at Carnegie Mellon and published by my colleague Ozan Baris. We used a distributed set of temperature sensors and model predictive control to retrofit my home's thermostat and optimize thermal comfort of our single-zone multi-room house.

mmWave Radar Metal Detection

I developed a program to fuse readings from multiple mmWave radars to detect and locate metalic objects in a wide area. This was preliminary research done in collaboration with Carnegie Mellon's WiTech lab for more efficient weapons screening technology planned for airports and large events.

Thermal Regulator Circuit for NASA MoonRanger

I designed an analog, schmitt trigger-based circuit for maintaining optimal thermal conditions of avionic systems for NASA's MoonRanger project in collaboration with Carnegie Mellon University.
